Connecting Infotograf
to the Fediverse

How I opened up a photo-sharing app to the decentralised social web — and what that means for your photos.

Chapter 1

The Vision

When I built Infotograf, I had one goal: bring back what made photo sharing brilliant. A chronological feed. Classic filters. Just photos, and the people who take them.

From the start, I wanted Infotograf to be part of something bigger — a connected world where your photos can travel beyond any single platform. The Fediverse made that possible. It's a network of millions of people across Mastodon, Pixelfed, Misskey, and hundreds of other platforms, all speaking the same open protocol: ActivityPub.

With federation, your Infotograf photos reach people wherever they are. Someone on Mastodon can follow you, like your photos, and comment — all from their own platform. And you see it all right in your Infotograf feed. It's social media the way it ought to work: open, connected, and yours.

Chapter 2

What Is the Fediverse?

Think of it like email. You can have a Gmail account and send a message to someone on Outlook. Different providers, same protocol. It simply works.

The Fediverse works the same way — but for social media. Each platform is its own independent server, but they all speak a common language called ActivityPub. A photo on Infotograf can appear in the feed of someone on Mastodon. A like from Pixelfed shows up as a notification in your Infotograf app. Everything connects seamlessly.

Fediverse Mastodon → Infotograf → Pixelfed
Different apps, same protocol

Your identity, your photos, your followers — they belong to you and travel with you. The Fediverse is an open network, built on open standards, where every platform can talk to every other. That's the power of connected, decentralised social media.

Chapter 3

How I Built It

Implementing ActivityPub from scratch was one of the most rewarding challenges I've taken on as a developer. There's a W3C specification, reference implementations to study, and an incredibly welcoming community. I read the spec, studied how Mastodon and Pixelfed handle federation, and built it piece by piece.

1

Discovery — WebFinger & NodeInfo

The first step was making Infotograf discoverable. When someone on Mastodon searches for @quentin@infotograf.com, their server asks infotograf.com "who is this person?" via WebFinger — a simple, standardised JSON lookup. I set up the backend to respond to these queries and configured Vercel to route discovery requests through to our API server on Railway.

2

Actor Profiles & Cryptographic Keys

Every Infotograf user who opts into the Fediverse receives an Actor profile — a JSON-LD document describing who they are, where to send them activities, and their RSA public key. Each user gets a unique RSA-2048 keypair. These keys are the foundation of trust across the network: every message Infotograf sends is cryptographically signed, so receiving servers can verify it genuinely came from us.

3

Sending Photos Out

When you post a photo on Infotograf, the backend wraps it in an ActivityPub Create/Note activity — complete with image attachment and caption — and delivers it to every remote follower's inbox. Each delivery is signed with HTTP Signatures and queued through Redis for reliability. If a server is temporarily unreachable, we retry automatically with exponential backoff until it goes through.

4

Receiving Interactions

When someone on Mastodon likes your photo or leaves a comment, their server sends an activity to your inbox on Infotograf. We verify the cryptographic signature, parse the activity, and process it — a Like becomes a like count, a reply becomes a comment, a Follow becomes a follow request. It all shows up in your notifications with a small Fediverse badge so you know it came from the wider network.

Chapter 4

What You'll Experience

Search Across Platforms

Find and follow anyone on the Fediverse directly from Infotograf. Search @user@mastodon.social and their profile appears instantly.

One Unified Feed

Posts from Fediverse users appear in your chronological feed alongside local posts. A subtle purple badge marks federated content so you always know where it came from.

Cross-Platform Notifications

Likes, comments, and new followers from Mastodon, Pixelfed, and others arrive in your regular notification feed — tagged with a Fediverse badge.

Remote Profiles

Tap a federated user to view their full profile — photo grid, follower count, and a distinctive purple glow on their avatar. Follow them with a single tap.

Fediverse Explore

A dedicated explore screen lets you browse popular servers, discover photographers on Pixelfed, and find communities that match your interests.

You're in Control

Everything is opt-in. You choose whether to share to the Fediverse, allow remote followers, or auto-accept follows. Private accounts stay private — always.

Chapter 5

The Journey

The Spark

I was sitting in a cafe in early 2026 when the idea clicked. I'd built exactly the photo app I wanted to use — chronological, simple, beautiful. And I thought: what if it could connect to the wider world? What if your Infotograf photos could reach people on Mastodon, Pixelfed, and beyond — and theirs could reach you? That was the moment I decided to build federation.

Reading the Spec

The ActivityPub W3C specification is dense. It took me days to fully grasp the difference between an Actor and an Activity, between an Inbox and an Outbox. I read Mastodon's source code. I read Pixelfed's. I traced HTTP requests with curl. Gradually, the protocol started making sense — it's elegant, actually. Just JSON documents, sent between servers, signed with cryptographic keys.

The First Handshake

The moment I searched @quentin@infotograf.com on Mastodon and saw my profile appear — my avatar, my bio, my photos — I got chills. Two completely independent servers, built by different people, using different languages, talking to each other through an open standard. That's the internet I believe in.

The Hard Parts

HTTP Signatures were the trickiest bit. One wrong byte in the signing string and the whole handshake fails silently. I spent three days on a bug that turned out to be a single trailing newline character. But I learnt something valuable: the Fediverse is built on trust, and trust requires precision.

Audio — Our Unique Touch

Infotograf's signature feature is audio — ambient sound captured with every photo, and voice clips where you talk about the moment. This is something uniquely ours. When your photo travels to Mastodon, they'll see the image and caption beautifully. But the full audio experience — the cafe rain, your voice describing the light — that's something special you get on Infotograf. It's the extra layer that makes our app worth opening.

What It Means

Building on the Fediverse means choosing a future where social media is open and interconnected. Where your photos can travel freely across platforms. Where apps of all sizes can coexist and collaborate through shared, open standards. That's the internet we're building towards.

Chapter 6

Your Fediverse Identity

yourname @yourname@infotograf.com Discoverable from any Fediverse platform

When you enable the Fediverse on Infotograf, your identity becomes @yourname@infotograf.com — recognised across every Fediverse platform. Anyone on Mastodon, Pixelfed, Misskey, Friendica, or any other ActivityPub-compatible service can find you, follow you, and interact with your photos.

You needn't create another account or understand the protocol. Simply switch it on in settings, and the entire decentralised social web opens up to you.
