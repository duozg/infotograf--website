Bringing Infotograf
to the Fediverse

How I connected a small photo-sharing app to the decentralized social web — and why it matters.

Chapter 1

Why the Fediverse?

When I built Infotograf, I had one goal: bring back what made photo sharing great before algorithms took over. A chronological feed. Classic filters. No ads, no reels, no shopping. Just photos.

But I kept running into the same problem every small social app faces — the network effect. People loved the idea, but their friends were all on Instagram. How do you build a community when you're starting from zero?

The Fediverse answered that question. Instead of competing with Instagram for users, Infotograf could connect to an existing network of millions — Mastodon, Pixelfed, Misskey, and hundreds of other platforms — all speaking the same protocol: ActivityPub.

Your photos wouldn't be trapped inside Infotograf. They could travel. Someone on Mastodon could follow you, like your photos, and comment — all without leaving their own platform. And you'd see it all right in your Infotograf feed.

Chapter 2

What is the Fediverse?

Think of it like email. You can have a Gmail account and send a message to someone on Outlook. Different providers, same protocol. Nobody owns email. It just works.

The Fediverse works the same way — but for social media. Each platform is its own independent server, but they all speak a common language called ActivityPub. A photo on Infotograf can appear in the feed of someone on Mastodon. A like from Pixelfed shows up as a notification in your Infotograf app.

Email Gmail → Outlook → Yahoo
Different apps, same protocol
Fediverse Mastodon → Infotograf → Pixelfed
Different apps, same protocol

No single company controls it. No single company can shut it down. Your identity, your photos, your followers — they're yours. If you ever leave Infotograf, your Fediverse connections can follow you to wherever you go next.

Chapter 3

How I Built It

Implementing ActivityPub from scratch was one of the most challenging things I've done as a developer. There's no SDK, no plug-and-play library. You read the W3C spec, you study how Mastodon does it, and you build it piece by piece.

1

Discovery — WebFinger & NodeInfo

First, I made Infotograf discoverable. When a Mastodon user searches for @quentin@infotograf.com, their server asks infotograf.com "who is this person?" via WebFinger — a simple JSON lookup. I set up the backend to answer these questions and configured Vercel to forward discovery requests to our API server on Railway.

2

Actor Profiles & Cryptographic Keys

Every Infotograf user who opts into the Fediverse gets an Actor profile — a JSON-LD document that describes who they are, where to send them activities, and their RSA public key. I generate unique RSA-2048 keypairs for each user. These keys are the foundation of trust: every message we send is cryptographically signed.

3

Sending Photos Out

When you post a photo on Infotograf, the backend wraps it in an ActivityPub Create/Note activity — complete with image attachment and caption — and delivers it to every remote follower's inbox. Each delivery is signed with HTTP Signatures and queued through Redis for reliability. If a server is down, we retry with exponential backoff.

4

Receiving Interactions

When someone on Mastodon likes your photo or leaves a comment, their server sends an activity to your inbox on Infotograf. We verify the cryptographic signature, parse the activity, and process it — a Like becomes a like count, a reply becomes a comment, a Follow becomes a follow request. It all shows up in your notifications with a small Fediverse badge so you know it came from the wider network.

Chapter 4

What You'll Experience

Search Across Platforms

Find and follow anyone on the Fediverse right from Infotograf. Search @user@mastodon.social and their profile appears.

One Unified Feed

Posts from Fediverse users appear right in your chronological feed, mixed naturally with local posts. A subtle purple badge marks federated content.

Cross-Platform Notifications

Likes, comments, and new followers from Mastodon, Pixelfed, and others arrive in your regular notification feed — tagged with a Fediverse badge.

Remote Profiles

Tap a federated user to see their profile, complete with photo grid, follower count, and a purple glow on their avatar. Follow them with one tap.

Fediverse Explore

A dedicated explore screen lets you browse popular servers, discover photographers on Pixelfed, and find communities that match your interests.

Privacy First

Everything is opt-in. You choose whether to share to the Fediverse, allow remote followers, or auto-accept follows. Private accounts stay private — always.

Chapter 5

The Journey

The Spark

I was sitting in a cafe in early 2026 when I realized something. I'd built exactly the photo app I wanted to use — chronological, simple, beautiful. But my friends were still on Instagram. I couldn't ask them all to switch. I needed a bridge, not a wall.

Reading the Spec

The ActivityPub W3C specification is dense. It took me days to understand the difference between an Actor and an Activity, between an Inbox and an Outbox. I read Mastodon's source code. I read Pixelfed's. I traced HTTP requests with curl. Slowly, the protocol started making sense — it's elegant, actually. Just JSON documents, sent between servers, signed with cryptographic keys.

The First Handshake

The moment I searched @quentin@infotograf.com on Mastodon and saw my profile appear — my avatar, my bio, my photos — I got chills. Two completely independent servers, built by different people, using different languages, talking to each other through an open standard. That's the internet I believe in.

The Hard Parts

HTTP Signatures were the hardest. One wrong byte in the signing string and the whole handshake fails silently. Mastodon returns 401, no explanation. I spent three days on a bug that turned out to be a single trailing newline. But I learned: the Fediverse is built on trust, and trust requires precision.

Audio on the Fediverse

Infotograf's unique feature is audio — ambient sound captured with every photo, and voice clips where you talk about the moment. This doesn't have a Fediverse equivalent. Mastodon users will see the photo and caption, but they won't hear the cafe rain or your voice describing the light. And that's okay. The audio experience stays special to Infotograf. It's a reason to use the app, not just the protocol.

What It Means

Building on the Fediverse means I'm choosing a future where no single company owns social media. Where your photos aren't held hostage by a platform's terms of service. Where small apps like Infotograf can exist alongside giants — not by competing for users, but by sharing an open network. That's the internet we deserve.

Chapter 6

Your Fediverse Identity

yourname @yourname@infotograf.com Searchable from any Fediverse platform

When you enable the Fediverse on Infotograf, your identity becomes @yourname@infotograf.com — recognized across every Fediverse platform. Anyone on Mastodon, Pixelfed, Misskey, Friendica, or any other ActivityPub-compatible service can find you, follow you, and interact with your photos.

You don't need to create another account. You don't need to understand the protocol. You just flip a switch in settings, and the entire decentralized social web opens up.
